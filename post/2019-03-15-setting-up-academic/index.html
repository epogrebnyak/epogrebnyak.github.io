Setting Up Academic Theme

Mar 15, 2019 2 min read

Academic theme is great, well-thought in design and popular with users. I’m migrating from a Jekyll-based CV theme to Academic.

CV was a good start and helped to sort out information, but did never lead to new contacts and was not extensible, did set up once, rarely updated). I will phase it out gradually.

Some personal notes on setup:

  • contacts section configurtion explained in Leslie Myint blog post
  • added StackOverflow icon
  • moved several files (including .sh) to ignore folder - makes cleaner root of repository
  • disabling people and groups - probably good for another, collaborative site
  • what is the difference between tags and categories?
  • https://formspree.io contact for will need activation (both on localhost and your server)
  • add personal photo as avatar.png

I disabled following sections:

  • publications, featured (duplicated, not filled yet)
  • skills (great look, had it in Jekyll CV, but how do you access + must move off the front page)
  • accomplishments (must put a list of econometrics courses in)
  • talks (would be nice to recollect!)
  • experience (to fill later, was not filled in a CV)
  • what is hero for?

I first disabled the blog too and thought it was such an archaic activity - writing a blog, but now I see it as a form of notetaking and knowledge management. And the best way of managing some knowledge is:

The most effective way to share knowledge within your organization is to share it with the whole world

— Chris Whong (@chris_whong) March 14, 2019
